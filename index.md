<section class="entries" aria-labelledby="entries-title">
  <h2 id="entries-title" class="entries__title">
    <span>Entries in this log book</span>
    <em>VI · सूची</em>
  </h2>

  <article class="entry">
    <span class="entry__num">01</span>
    <h3 class="entry__head">Vehicles<em>गाड़ियाँ</em></h3>
    <p class="entry__body">Every car, bike, or scooter you own. <strong>Photo, registration number, make, model, year, current odometer.</strong> One tap to add another. Soft-delete, so an old vehicle never truly disappears from the history.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="entry">
    <span class="entry__num">02</span>
    <h3 class="entry__head">Fuel-ups<em>ईंधन</em></h3>
    <p class="entry__body">Log each fill-up and GaadiLog <strong>auto-calculates km/l and ₹/km.</strong> Full-tank and partial-fill rules are handled for you — the math just works. A km/l trend chart sits on the vehicle's page.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="entry">
    <span class="entry__num">03</span>
    <h3 class="entry__head">Service log<em>सेवा</em></h3>
    <p class="entry__body">Workshop visits, parts, costs, and <strong>multiple invoice photos</strong> per visit. Running cost totals roll up per vehicle, so the next service quote has a number to anchor against.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="entry">
    <span class="entry__num">04</span>
    <h3 class="entry__head">Documents<em>दस्तावेज़</em></h3>
    <p class="entry__body">RC, Insurance, and PUC scans with expiry dates. The app warns you the moment something is about to expire — <strong>30, 7, and 1 days before, and on the day itself.</strong></p>
  </article>

  <article class="entry">
    <span class="entry__num">05</span>
    <h3 class="entry__head">Reminders<em>स्मरण</em></h3>
    <p class="entry__body">Custom reminders by <strong>date or by odometer reading.</strong> Optional recurrence for service intervals. All scheduling is local — your phone fires the notifications; no server knows what you've set.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="entry">
    <span class="entry__num">06</span>
    <h3 class="entry__head">Export<em>निर्यात</em></h3>
    <p class="entry__body">One-tap <strong>CSV export</strong> per vehicle or for every vehicle at once. A <strong>PDF "Service History" report</strong> you can hand to a buyer, a workshop, or an insurer. Your data, your file, your share sheet.</p>
  </article>
</section>

<section class="assurance" aria-labelledby="assurance-title">
  <span class="assurance__label">A promise</span>
  <p id="assurance-title" class="assurance__big">Your data stays on your <em>phone.</em></p>
  <p class="assurance__body">No accounts. No cloud sync. No ad trackers. Everything you log — vehicles, fuel-ups, services, photos — lives only in GaadiLog's private storage on your device. Delete the app and the data goes with it; there is genuinely no remote copy. The one thing that can optionally leave the device is anonymous usage analytics (event names like <code>fuel_logged</code>, never your data) — off by default in Settings → Privacy.</p>
